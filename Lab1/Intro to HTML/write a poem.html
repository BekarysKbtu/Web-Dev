Whispers of Dawn

In the hush before the dawn,
Where dreams and shadows meet,
The world is wrapped in velvet night,
A lullaby so sweet.
Stars like whispers trace the sky,
Their tales of ages spun,
Of love and loss, and whispered dreams,
Beneath the waiting sun.
The moon, a silver sentinel,
Keeps watch upon the deep,
While waves caress the sandy shore,
In rhythms soft and steep.
Through the trees, a tender breeze,
A sigh of nature’s grace,
It dances through the leaves and limbs,
A lover’s soft embrace.
Then comes the blush of morning light,
A promise gently cast,
That night will fade, and day will rise,
Though shadows always last.
For in the quiet of the dawn,
Where light and dark entwine,
We find the beauty of the world,
In every fleeting line.
